## About me

I build machine learning and computational methods to understand how the brain works — and to translate that understanding into clinical tools. My work sits at the intersection of **neuroscience**, **AI/ML**, and **medicine**, and spans:

- **Neuroadaptive Bayesian optimisation** — closed-loop systems that fuse real-time neuroimaging with ML to personalise experiments and interventions
- **Brain network analysis** — functional MRI, dynamic connectivity, and the geometry of brain organisation
- **Biomarker discovery** — applying ML to psychiatric and neurological disorders (ASD, epilepsy, psychosis, traumatic brain injury)
- **Reproducibility & robust methods** — multiverse analysis, entropy-based approaches, and rigorous pipeline evaluation

I'm based at the [Institute of Psychiatry, Psychology & Neuroscience (IoPPN)](https://www.kcl.ac.uk/ioppn), King's College London.
